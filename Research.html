Research

Tournament-experiments studying performance under real competition.

My work sits at the intersection of esports, human-computer interaction (HCI), and game systems. I am especially interested in what changes when you move from a lab study to a competitive environment - expertise, incentives, pressure, and audience.

Research themes

1) Tournament-experiments

Controlled studies embedded inside real esports tournaments. The goal is to keep the benefits of experimentation (causal inference, counterfactual comparison) while embracing real competitive context.

2) Expertise, incentives, and pressure

Competitive play changes behavior and attention. I study how expertise interacts with game parameters and how external motivation (prizes, rankings, audience) shapes performance.

3) Perception and performance in games

I investigate how display and system factors (latency, frame rate, and other settings) affect what players perceive and how they perform, especially at high skill.

Selected projects

  • Field tournament pilots - designs that balance fairness and fun with experimental control.
  • Latency and perceptual thresholds - measuring correctness and confidence under competitive conditions.
  • Competitive configuration studies - how players tune settings and what those choices do to outcomes.
